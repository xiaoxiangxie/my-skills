# Social Feed Ad Patterns

Use this reference for short-video, social, creator, UGC, live shopping, community, or content-feed products. These ads should feel native to fast vertical feeds, not like SaaS explainers or slide decks.

## Creative Bias

Lead with the behavior the user already understands: swipe, watch, react, create, go live, shop, save, or share. Show the app as a moving feed surface with real or harvested screenshots inside a phone frame, then layer kinetic UI gestures around it.

Good visual ideas:

- A feed card snaps into the phone while background cards keep scrolling.
- Creator imagery cuts in like a clip, then collapses back into the app UI.
- Sound, effects, live, shop, or comment stickers pop in rhythmically.
- A logo glitch or thumb-swipe gesture triggers the next scene.
- Social proof lands as quick stickers, not slow stat slides.
- CTA feels like the next swipe or next open action.

Avoid:

- Static screenshots with text explanations.
- Feature-by-feature slides.
- Generic gradients without harvested app assets.
- Long paragraphs about the community or algorithm.
- Overclaiming cultural impact, earnings, views, or creator outcomes unless user-supplied and approved.

## 15s Structure

- 0-2s: Product visible immediately. Use logo glitch, thumb swipe, or feed snap with a 3-6 word hook.
- 2-6s: Phone/feed motion. Rotate between 2-3 screenshots or simulated content cards.
- 6-11s: Creator or content payoff. Use source-supported modes such as LIVE, Shop, sound, effects, offline, groups, or comments.
- 11-15s: CTA. Keep app icon, action button, and one proof cluster visible.

## Motion Requirements

Include at least three of:

- Continuous vertical feed drift in the background.
- Phone frame swipe or content swap every 0.8-1.5 seconds.
- Glitch, chromatic shadow, or snap zoom on logo/headline.
- Stickers or UI chips that pop with `spring()`.
- Flash cuts between scenes.
- CTA pulse that does not move surrounding layout.

## Asset Use

Harvest and store available app icon, Play/App Store screenshots, feature graphics, OG images, and brand colors under `public/<brand>/`. Prefer app screenshots and source feature graphics over generic stock.

When source screenshots include their own text, do not place new text directly on top. Use a phone top/bottom overlay, crop, dark gradient, or choose a quieter screenshot area.

## Copy Rules

Use short feed-native lines:

- `Swipe into what's next.`
- `Your feed keeps moving.`
- `Sound on.`
- `Live now.`
- `Shop the moment.`
- `Open the next scroll.`

Proof copy should be tagged and source-backed, for example store rating, reviews, downloads, ranking badge, or user-supplied creator/community proof.
